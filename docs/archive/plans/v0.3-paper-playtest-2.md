# v0.3 Paper Playtest — Second Pass

Validates the updated model with all 5 gap-resolutions baked in:
- Damage tiers + HP clamping
- Medic + partial healing + Black Market heal
- Modifier ownership with swap action (no orphans; seize reconciliation)
- Heat coefficients + rarity-weighted bribes + raid escalation
- Mythic engagement (flip on kill, not via Coup)

## Setup

**Difficulty**: Medium. 4 turfs per side. 3 actions/turn, 5 on the
first turn of each new active turf.

**Both players post-curation decks** (player A has leaned into
durability; AI B has leaned into offense):

**Player A (20 toughs + mods):**
- 15 common toughs (mix kings_row / iron_devils)
- 3 uncommon toughs (includes a FIELD_MEDIC uncommon tough — heals 1 HP/turn to toughs sharing the turf)
- 1 rare tough
- 1 **legendary tough — "The Medic"** (unique, full heal on wounded target once per war)
- Weapons: 3 common LACERATE, 1 common BRACE, 1 common PARRY, 1 uncommon OVERWATCH
- Drugs: 2 common RUSH, 1 common FORTIFY, 1 rare NUMB, 1 rare PATCHUP (+1 HP/turn to owner tough)
- Currency: $100 ×3, $1000 ×2

**AI B (20 toughs + mods):**
- 17 commons (spread affiliations)
- 2 rares (includes Coup-legendary from earlier — wait, Coup's dropped, so rare OVERWATCH instead)
- 1 **legendary tough** (base legendary, has CHAIN_STRIKE ability: hits top + one below in same resolution)
- Weapons: 2 common LACERATE, 1 common PARRY, 1 uncommon BERSERK weapon variant
- Drugs: 2 common RUSH, 1 uncommon FORTIFY, 1 rare LAUNDER-able equiv. Wait — LAUNDER is currency. Let's say: 1 rare LOW_PROFILE drug (halves heat contribution for owner)
- Currency: $100 ×4, $1000 ×1

**Shared state:**
- Heat: 0.0
- Black Market: empty
- Holding (A): empty
- Holding (B): empty
- Neither side owns a Mythic yet.

**Mythic pool** (10 pool):
- Unassigned: 10 mythics remain. Our trace below will NOT feature a
  Mythic naturally appearing — we'll inject one via a seed condition
  mid-game to validate the Mythic flip-on-defeat mechanic.

     - **Does A choose to bribe?** This is a decision point, not

  4. Weapons: Delta has no weapon. FIELD_MEDIC has no weapon. Alpha

  - P < R. Damage tier: P < R → glance. Damage = 0. Busted.

  1. Affiliations: kings_row vs iron_devils (Warlord). Rival.

  4. Weapons: FIELD_MEDIC no weapon. Warlord no weapon. Skip.

  - A: STAY OPEN (same reasoning).

     face-up (just played this turn), so there's no "deeper revealed
     tough" that isn't already visible. CLOSE succeeds, Warlord +
     Delta both flip face-down... wait.
  - Clarification: **close-ranks only flips the CURRENT TOP face-down**.
    Cards below stay as they were. So if B closes, Delta flips face-down,

## Observations

### What worked
- **Damage tiers + HP clamping**: wounded toughs meaningfully weaker.
  Romeo's clamp kept him combat-viable but strictly worse. Satisfying.
- **Modifier swap**: the action-cost swap enabled tactical repositioning
  (moving LACERATE to FIELD_MEDIC for offense). The face-up-on-active
  rule works.
- **Seize reconciliation**: dead tough's modifiers → Market. Clean.
- **Bribes**: lots of money spent saving tops. Feels appropriate —
  money is a real defensive resource.
- **FIELD_MEDIC + PATCHUP**: never activated in this trace, but the
  position of them created "threat of healing" that influenced B's
  play. Good. (In a longer trace with A-side wounds, the healing chain
  would shine.)
- **Mythic STRIKE_TWO**: overwhelming but not auto-win. BODYGUARD
  (rare drug) made it feel earned.
- **Heat accumulation**: reached near-raid levels a few times. Never
  triggered. Raid was a background tension. Correct.

### What didn't work / problems
- **Too many busted strikes**. Over 10 turns, maybe 4 kills happened.
  The cumulative R + loyal bonus stacking makes attacks hard.
  - **Proposed fix**: add a **"minimum power threshold"** — if
    attacker has any weapon + any drug, their base P floors at 1.5 ×
    rolled_P for damage calc. Encourages equipping and de-values raw
    unequipped attacks.
  - Alt: **cumulative R should NOT apply to damage calc** — only
    target's own R matters. That's actually already in the playtest
    (I was using target's own R for tangible combat). Cumulative only
    for Pass 1 dominance ordering. Confirmed good.
- **Close ranks decision is confusing**. When to close vs stay open is
  unclear. My simplification ("close only when no face-up tough in
  stack") reduces agency but increases clarity. Consider: make close
  always available but with consequences (revealed toughs get hidden
  by close action = illegal, so close fails).
- **Bribes always save the day on high-cash turfs**. $500 flat
  threshold means defense-by-currency is trivial. Two fixes:
  1. **Rarity-scaled bribe thresholds**: attacking a legendary costs
     $2000, mythic costs $5000.
  2. **Bribe is probabilistic**: even with $500, there's a 70% success
     chance; with $1000, 85%; with $2000, 95%.
  - Lean: probabilistic (fix 2). Adds uncertainty. Retains agency.
- **Black Market heal too rigid**. Romeo couldn't heal because B had
  no matching currency or commons to spend. Very punishing.
  - **Alt**: allow "partial heal" at Market — spend 1 common tough to
    heal 2 HP to any wounded tough. Cheaper option for common toughs.
- **Modifier replacement on play**: A played BRACE on FIELD_MEDIC
  which already had LACERATE. Auto-replace pushed LACERATE to Market.
  Feels punishing. Alt: **play on a full slot is rejected** — forces
  player to swap first. More rigorous, prevents accidental loss.

### Gaps surfaced
1. **Tie-break rule for same dominance**: proposed alphabetical by side
   (A first). Is that right, or should be first-mover (the player
   who took the first action this turn)?
2. **Freelancer + loyal stack**: Freelancer counted as "neutral to
   everyone" so didn't break loyalty bonus. Need to formalize in RULES.
3. **Cumulative R usage**: confirmed only Pass 1 dominance. NOT for
   tangible damage calc. Need to document clearly.
4. **Seize reconciliation order**: when a tough dies, what happens to
   their modifiers BEFORE the new top shifts up? Current trace: modifier
   → Market immediately. Alternative: modifier stays in stack and
   shifts up WITH the next tough (becoming their new equipment,
   respecting slot limits).
   - My lean changed from earlier: **modifier goes to Market on tough
     death**. Cleaner. No "inheriting gear from corpse" thematic
     weirdness. Confirm in RULES.
5. **Modifier slot capacity on seize**: if a modifier orphans and the
   new top is at capacity, we said earlier "excess to Market" but in
   this playtest I sent to Market immediately on death, regardless
   of new top state. Cleaner. Confirm.

### Refinements before RULES.md
- **Bribe probabilistic**: $500 = 70%, $1000 = 85%, $2000 = 95%, $5000 = 99%.
- **Black Market partial heal**: 1 common tough → heal 2 HP.
- **Mod-play-on-full-slot**: reject action (player must swap first).
- **Cumulative R only for dominance, not damage**: formalize.
- **Seize → Market always**: formalize.
- **Close-ranks simplified rule**: close always succeeds when chosen;
  if a face-up deeper tough exists, they shift to top (no close).
  Reverse: close is impossible if you have revealed deeper toughs.
  Player-friendly: the button "closes ranks" simply isn't clickable
  when impossible.

### Unchanged from first pass
- Single-lane progression.
- 5/4/3 action budget structure.
- Heat coefficients placeholder.
- Mythic flip only on kill (no Coup).
- Sudden Death removed.
- Victory decisiveness + pack rewards.
- AI opponent profile + mirror progression.
