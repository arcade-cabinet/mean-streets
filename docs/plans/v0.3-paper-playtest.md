# v0.3 Paper Playtest

End-to-end walkthrough validating the v0.3 model before RULES.md
rewrite. Tests: single-lane turf progression, closed ranks shift-up,
Black Market trade, Holding→bribe, heat accumulation, raid resolution
before combat, and a damage-tracked assault on a Legendary (not a
Mythic here — Mythics are tested in a second trace below).

## Setup

**Difficulty**: Medium. 4 turfs each, 3 actions/turn, 5 on the first
turn of each new active turf.

**Player A starter decks** (post-starter-grant + a few wins of curation):
- 20 toughs (base commons mostly; 2 base-rare; 1 base-legendary)
- 5 weapons (common LACERATE, common PARRY, common BRACE, uncommon BERSERK, rare OVERWATCH)
- 5 drugs (common RUSH, common FORTIFY, common SUPPRESS, uncommon PAINKILLERS, rare NUMB)
- 5 currency ($100 ×3, $1000 ×2)

Same for AI B.

**Shared game state:**
- Heat: 0.0 / 1.0
- Black Market: empty
- Holding (player A): empty
- Holding (player B): empty
- Police raid probability per turn: f(heat, difficulty)

**Active lane:** A's turf 1 vs B's turf 1. Reserves: A has 3 behind, B has 3 behind.

## Symbols in the trace

```
[T] = tough    [W] = weapon    [D] = drug    [C] = currency
(face-up)  {face-down}  /closed-ranks/
× = dead    ↑ = promoted up    ⟳ = retreated
→MKT = sent to Black Market
→HLD = sent to Holding
```

**Stack at end**: `[Alpha(5/5) + LACERATE(+1 atk face-down to B)]`
**Visible to B**: Alpha face-up, one face-down modifier tucked under.

### Resolution phase (turn 2)

- **Raid check**: heat 0.10, raid prob ~1%. Roll d100, say we rolled
  54. No raid.
- **Combat resolution**:
  - **Pass 1 — gross dominance**:
    - A's attacker power: Alpha(5) + LACERATE(+1 tangible) = **6**.
      Currency doesn't contribute to raw power.
    - A's defender resistance: Alpha(5) = **5**.
    - B's attacker power: Delta(5) = **5**. (Bravo's BRACE contributes
      to DEFENSE only; Bravo isn't the striker.)
    - B's defender resistance: Delta(5) + Bravo(6 — wait, base was 6,
      on top tough: only top tough's R counts for default strike target?)
      Actually — **strikes target top tough by default**. So defender
      R = Delta's R = **5**. Bravo's +BRACE is for Bravo's own defense
      if Bravo is struck. It doesn't help Delta.
    - A attacker 6 vs B defender 5: A hits, dominance delta = +1.
    - B attacker 5 vs A defender 5: B hits, dominance delta = 0.
    - **A has higher swing. A resolves first.**
  - **Pass 2 — priority-ordered modifier chain (A's strike first)**:
    1. **Affiliations**: A (kings_row) attacks B top (neon_snakes).
       Graph: no loyalty match (no bonus). No rival (no penalty). No
       buffer needed. Skip.
    2. **Currency pressure**: A has $1000, B has $0 on top. Bribe
       threshold = $500 (defender spends currency to cancel). B has no
       currency → no bribe available. Skip.
    3. **Drug influence**: neither side has drugs yet. Skip.
    4. **Weapon strength**: A's LACERATE = +1 (tangible, already
       applied). B's face-down modifier was BRACE which is tucked under
       Bravo (not Delta). Delta has no weapon — no PARRY / EVASION.
       No counter. A's strike proceeds.
  - **Tangible combat**:
    - Attacker power 6 vs defender R 5. Kill threshold: P ≥ R.
      Delta dies.
    - **New mechanic — damage tracking**: Delta's rolled-common
      resistance = 5. Damage points a common tough can absorb before
      death = its rolled resistance × 1.0 damage capacity. A dealt
      P-minus-R = 1 damage over threshold. With damage tracking:
      - If strike power ≥ 1.5 × defender R, instant kill.
      - If strike power ≥ R but < 1.5×R, partial kill: damage = P-R,
        defender takes wound token.
      - If strike power < R, no effect (busted).
      - Actually — simpler: **damage = attacker_power - defender_R**,
        clamped to ≥ 1 if P ≥ R. Tough has HP = rolled_R. When HP ≤ 0,
        dies. So Delta takes 1 dmg, HP 5 → 4. **Delta wounded, not
        dead.**
      - **Note**: this contradicts existing sim where P ≥ R = kill
        outright. v0.3 should adopt HP model for richer combat. See
        decision point below.
  - **Pass 1-2 for B's strike (B resolves second)**:
    1. B (iron_devils via Bravo — but Delta is the attacker!)  wait,
       who's actually attacking? The TOP tough is the attacker.
       Delta (neon_snakes) is the attacker. So affiliations: neon_snakes
       vs kings_row. Graph says neutral. No bonus/penalty.
    2. Currency: A has $1000 on Alpha. B has $0. B can't bribe.
    3. Drugs: neither.
    4. Weapons: B's face-down BRACE is on Bravo, not Delta. Delta has
       no weapon. No PARRY. A has LACERATE on Alpha — LACERATE is
       offensive (+1 atk); doesn't help defense.
  - **Tangible combat**:
    - B attacker P 5 vs A defender R 5. Damage = 5 - 5 = 0 → clamp to 1.
      Alpha takes 1 dmg, HP 5 → 4. **Alpha wounded.**

- **Post-resolution state**:
  - A's active turf: `[Alpha(HP 4/5) + LACERATE + $1000]`. Top wounded.
  - B's active turf: `[Delta(HP 4/5), Bravo(6) + BRACE(+1)]`. Top wounded.
  - Heat recalculation: no change from combat. Still 0.10-ish.

- **Closed Ranks check (end of turn, per side)**:
  - A: ends turn with active tough (Alpha wounded but alive). **Option: close ranks or not.**
    - If A closes ranks (flips Alpha face-down): defensive bonus on
      Medium = +35% resistance. But: Alpha has no revealed toughs
      behind. Allowed to close.
    - A **chooses to close ranks**. Alpha now face-down. The LACERATE
      and $1000 under it remain face-down too.
    - But wait — in v0.3, closing ranks is the DEFAULT at end-of-turn.
      Not an action. Unless you had a revealed tough behind, you
      close by default. OK, A closes.
  - B: has Delta (wounded) AND Bravo (revealed or not?).
    - Bravo was played first in turn 1, was the face-up top of turn 1.
      When Delta was played on top of Bravo in turn 2, Bravo shifted
      down. Is Bravo face-up or face-down now?
    - **Rule**: when a card shifts down (another card pushed on top of
      it), the shifted-down card RETAINS its face state. Bravo was
      face-up (as top) — stays face-up under Delta.
    - So B has Bravo face-up in stack. Therefore B **cannot close ranks**
      on Delta. Delta must stay face-up.
    - Actually — let me reconsider. The rule was: "if you have ANY
      revealed tough in your stack, you can't close ranks — instead,
      the topmost revealed tough shifts up to become the new top."
    - So B's turn ends: Delta is top, Bravo is revealed below. Can B
      close ranks? Per rule: NO. The topmost revealed tough (Bravo)
      would shift up. But Delta is ALSO face-up (it's a fresh top).
      Is Delta face-up at end of turn 2? YES — it was just played
      this turn. The ONLY way for a top to be face-down at end of
      turn is if it started the turn as a face-down card OR the
      player forces closed ranks. Delta was played this turn, so
      Delta is face-up.
    - Hmm, but the "all tops go face-down unless prior-revealed"
      rule was about closing ranks at end of turn. Let me re-read
      the principle...
    - The intent: **closed ranks is a deliberate posture at end of
      turn**. If you WANT to close ranks, you flip your current top
      face-down. BUT: you can't close ranks if you have a revealed
      tough deeper in the stack (because otherwise you'd be hiding
      info that's already out). Instead the deeper revealed tough
      shifts up to become the new face-up top.
    - **OK so the check at end of turn is**: "does the player have
      any face-up toughs in their stack besides the current top?"
      - B has Bravo face-up in stack (position 0). YES.
      - Therefore B cannot close ranks on Delta.
      - Bravo shifts up. But Delta is STILL face-up (just played).
        What happens to Delta? Two options:
        - **Option A**: Delta moves to Bravo's old position (bottom
          of stack), stays face-up. Bravo + BRACE shift to top.
        - **Option B**: Delta shifts down one position only; Bravo
          shifts up to top. This is a "swap" — positional.
      - **Option B (one-up-one-down swap)** is cleaner. I'll go
        with that.
    - But wait — another thought: if a player chooses NOT to close
      ranks, the current top stays face-up. If there's a revealed
      tough below, it doesn't force-shift; only the close-ranks
      action triggers the shift-up. **Revised rule**:
      - At end of turn, each player chooses: CLOSE or STAY OPEN.
      - CLOSE: flips current top face-down IFF no revealed tough
        below; otherwise the topmost revealed tough shifts up and
        becomes face-up top (no close happens — the stack rotates).
      - STAY OPEN: current top stays face-up. No shift.
    - **For B on turn 2**: B chooses STAY OPEN. Delta face-up. Bravo
      face-up behind. Both visible to A.
    - **For A on turn 2**: A chooses CLOSE. Alpha flips face-down.
      No revealed tough below (Alpha is the only tough). Close
      succeeds.

**End of turn 2 state**:
- A: `/Alpha(HP 4/5)/ + {LACERATE} + {$1000}`. Closed ranks.
- B: `[Delta(HP 4/5), Bravo(HP 5/5) + BRACE(+1 def)]`. Both face-up.

**Heat**: recalc after turn 2.
- A: closed-ranks tough + $1000 + weapon = 0.02 + 0.04 = 0.06.
- B: 2 toughs + 1 weapon = 0.04.
- Total: **0.10**.

decides to send Alpha to **Holding** to try to bribe out the sick

### Player A actions (3 actions)

## Observations from the playtest

### What worked
- Single-lane engagement: UI is simple, focus is tactical.
- Closed Ranks + shift-up: works cleanly when a player chooses to
  close and there's a revealed deeper tough.
- Information asymmetry: face-down modifiers under tops create real
  uncertainty. B's PARRY-mkII surprise was satisfying.
- Heat + raid: randomness-guarded mechanic that can wipe both sides
  simultaneously. Creates tension around "how many legendaries / how
  much cash can I safely carry".
- Damage tracking: partial-kill moments felt richer than binary
  "kill or nothing". Wounded toughs add a layer.

### What didn't work / TBD
- **Attacking tough's modifiers**: clarified that mods belong to a
  specific tough, not the turf. A's LACERATE on Alpha doesn't help
  Kilo when Kilo becomes the top. This is actually fine — forces
  the player to stack multiple tough+weapon combos rather than just
  front-load one tough with everything.
- **Cumulative resistance for strike targets**: decided the target's
  own R is what the strike measures against; cumulative R affects
  dominance (pass 1 ordering) and closed-ranks bonus calc.
- **Busted-strike inflation**: too many "busted" outcomes = boring.
  Need to either (a) loosen the P ≥ R threshold slightly (make
  wounds more common), or (b) give players better tools to amplify
  atk.
- **Black Market rarity gate**: trading a common tough for a rare
  requires 4 commons. That's steep for early game. May need to
  adjust to 3-to-1 at least for common-to-uncommon.
- **Damage regeneration**: wounded toughs that survive — do they
  heal over time? Or is a wound permanent until death? My lean:
  permanent wound state. Healing abilities could be a rare drug.
- **Lockup release** when the top in custody vs. when the lockup
  period expires: current plan says release at end-of-turn after
  the lockup counter runs out. That seems right.
- **Queued strike source removed before combat**: dissolve the
  strike. Confirmed this feels right — it's a tactical
  consideration, not a bug.

### Gaps to resolve before RULES.md

1. **Damage/HP model** — add formally. Tough has `hp = rolled_R`.
   When struck and P ≥ R, damage = P - R (min 1). HP reduces. 0 HP
   = dead. Wounded toughs persist through turns but are weaker.
2. **Modifier ownership** — modifiers are owned by a specific
   tough, travel with them when retreated/shifted. When the owner
   dies, modifiers stay face-down in stack (orphan rule). When the
   owner goes to lockup, modifiers go with them (get seized on
   bribe or permanently lost on raid).
3. **Currency as turf-wide buffer** — clarify the rule. I propose:
   currency tied to a specific tough (per RULES.md so far), but
   when a rival-affiliated card is played on the turf, ANY currency
   on ANY tough in the turf can buffer it (currency is fluid on the
   turf). Cheapest currency consumed first. Confirm.
4. **Heat formula** — document the starting values:
   - Base heat per common tough: 0.005.
   - Base heat per uncommon: 0.01.
   - Base heat per rare: 0.02.
   - Base heat per legendary: 0.05.
   - Base heat per mythic: 0.10 (gets flagged by cops instantly).
   - Concentration spike: sum of currency denom / $10000 (so $1000
     = 0.1; $5000 = 0.5; $10000 = 1.0 which is max raid risk).
   - Raid probability per turn: `heat²` × difficulty coefficient.
5. **Mythic flip via Coup**: need to specify the Coup ability. My
   lean: a Legendary-base ability called "Coup" that when on a
   legendary-rolled weapon, during a queued strike, if the target
   is a mythic tough, 30% chance to flip instead of damage. The
   Mythic moves to the flipper's collection next turn. Super rare
   event by design.
